# Contributing

Contributions are welcome when they improve the rigor, portability, or clarity of the research workflow.

## Good contributions

- clearer evidence-grading rules;
- new public case studies;
- industry-chain mapping templates;
- falsification and monitoring frameworks;
- corrections to technical or factual claims;
- translations and examples for other research domains.

## Contribution requirements

1. Use public or explicitly authorized information only.
2. Cite primary sources whenever possible.
3. Label company-reported metrics and test conditions.
4. Separate facts, inference, and unverified claims.
5. Do not include client, account, portfolio, credential, or internal information.
6. Explain what changed and why it improves the method.

## Suggested pull request format

- **Problem:** What research weakness does this address?
- **Change:** What was added or modified?
- **Evidence:** Which primary sources or examples support it?
- **Privacy check:** Confirm no private or client information is included.

By contributing, you agree that your contribution may be distributed under the repository license.
